1 · Geometry on the grid

The golden ratio in the mark

  • bounding box W : H81 : 50 = φ
  • M width : C extension50 : 31 = φ
  • V-valley from cap (golden section)31 : 19 = φ
  • stroke weight = H / φ³50 / 4.24 = 12
  • M is a true square50 × 50

Everything derives from one number — the cap-height H = 50. The width is H·φ, the C extends the square M by H/φ, the inner V sits on the golden section, and the stroke is H/φ³. Define H, and the whole mark falls out. The 10° lean is the only non-derived, purely aesthetic move — applied last, as a shear.

2 · How to draw it by hand

Clear space & min size

Keep clear space ≥ the stroke weight on all sides. Legible to 16px; below that use the M alone.

Colour
Indigo · #6366f1 · primary
Ink · #08080a · on light
Paper · #ecedf2 · on dark

Always one flat colour. No gradients, no outline, no fill+stroke mixing.

Do / Don't
✓ keep the 10° lean & weight 12
✓ scale uniformly
✗ re-slant, condense or embolden
✗ round the corners or add a tile
✗ recolour with a gradient
